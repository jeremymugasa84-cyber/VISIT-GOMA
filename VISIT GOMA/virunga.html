Visit Virunga National park

The oldest national park in africa, home to mountain gorillas and more

Overview

Established in 1925, Virunga National Park is Africa's oldest national park and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Covering 790,000 hectares along the Albertine Rift on the border with Rwanda and Uganda, it is the most biologically diverse protected area on the continent. The park is home to 218 mammal species, 706 bird species, and over 2,077 plant species. It contains two active volcanoes, vast savannahs, tropical rainforests, and the last stronghold of the endangered mountain gorilla — all just 14 kilometres from Goma.

What's inside the park

Trek through dense rainforest to encounter habituated mountain gorilla families in their natural habitat. One of the rarest wildlife experiences on earth.

Important Info

  • Location: 14 km from Goma, eastern DRC
  • Park size: 790,000 hectares
  • Established: 1925, Africa's oldest national park
  • Min age: 15 years old for gorilla trekking
  • Best season: June to September (dry season)
  • Starting point: Grande Barrier post, Goma border
