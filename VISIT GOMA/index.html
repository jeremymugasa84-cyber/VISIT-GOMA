About Goma

Goma is a major city and the capital of North Kivu Province in eastern DRC, home to nearly 2 million people and sitting on the northern shore of Lake Kivu. It lies just 13–18 kilometres south of Mount Nyiragongo, one of the world's most active volcanoes, and borders the Rwandan city of Gisenyi, with both cities sitting within the Albertine Rift — the western arm of the East African Rift system. Goma serves as the main business hub for the region and is the closest town to Virunga National Park, home to endangered mountain gorillas, making it one of the DRC's top tourist destinations. Culturally, it has a lively arts and music scene — known for soukous, Congolese rumba, and jazz — and hosts the Festival Amani, a peace festival that brings together artists from across the Great Lakes region. However; the city has long been defined by conflict: in January 2025, M23 rebels seized Goma in just three days, taking control of a strategically vital city in a province rich in minerals like coltan, used in electronics and aerospace manufacturing.
