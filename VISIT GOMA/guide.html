How to Get to Goma

By Air

Goma International Airport (GOM) is located 1 km from the city center. Ethiopian Airlines flies directly from Addis Ababa (seasonal, October to March). For most international travelers, connections go through Addis Ababa, Nairobi, or Kinshasa. Domestic flights are available from Beni and Butembo with Busy Bee Congo.

From Kigali, Rwanda 🇷🇼

The most popular route. Buses like Atraco Express and Okapi Car run hourly from Kigali to the Gisenyi/Goma border, covering 160 km in approximately 3–4 hours on a paved road. Cross the border on foot at the Grande Barrière the border closes at 10:00 PM.

From Uganda 🇺🇬

Travel from Kisoro to the Cyanika border crossing about 60 km and under 2 hours. Bus operators like Jaguar Executive Coaches and Baby Coach run routes connecting Uganda through Rwanda into Goma. The journey passes through Rwanda making it a tri-country trip.

Border Crossing

The main crossing is the Grande Barrière between Goma and Gisenyi, Rwanda. Cross on foot the border closes at 10:00 PM, plan to arrive well before then. Have your passport, Congo visa, and yellow fever vaccination card ready at the checkpoint. Currency exchange is available at the border but rates are better in the city.

Visa Information

eVisa Online Application

The DRC now offers an eVisa processed within 72 hours. Apply at evisa.gouv.cd before you travel. The eVisa costs $300 and an additional $90 VAP border fee is paid on arrival at the checkpoint. Valid at all entry points Air, land, and sea.

Via Tour Operator

Virunga National Park and trusted local tour operators can act as visa facilitators especially useful for gorilla trekking and Nyiragongo permits. This is the easiest route for most tourists visiting the eastern DRC. Contact your guide or operator before you arrive.

Documents Required

Valid passport with at least 6 months validity and two blank visa pages. Yellow fever vaccination certificate (mandatory). Passport-size photo. Letter of invitation or hotel booking confirmation. Proof of sufficient funds for your stay.

Important Note

The DRC visa process for eastern Congo (North Kivu) requires special approval and can take up to a month through an embassy. Using a local tour operator or the eVisa system is strongly recommended for a faster, smoother process.

Practical Information

💰

Currency

US Dollars (USD) are widely accepted in Goma alongside Congolese Francs (CDF). Carry cash ATMs are limited. Small USD bills are very useful for day-to-day purchases.

🗣️

Language

French is the official language. Swahili is widely spoken on the streets of Goma. English is understood in tourist areas and by most guides.

💉

Health

Yellow fever vaccination is mandatory. Anti-malaria medication is strongly recommended. Bring personal medication and a basic first aid kit. Drink bottled water only.

🌤️

Best Time to Visit

June to September is the dry season best for hiking, gorilla trekking, and boat tours. January to February is also dry. Avoid March to May and October to November (heavy rains).

🚗

Getting Around

Boda-bodas (motorcycle taxis) are the most common way to get around Goma. Agree on the fare before you ride. For trips outside the city, use a trusted tour operator with a 4WD vehicle.

📱

Connectivity

Mobile data is available in Goma. Airtel and Vodacom Congo are the main networks. Wi-Fi is available at most hotels and some restaurants. Download offline maps before you arrive.
